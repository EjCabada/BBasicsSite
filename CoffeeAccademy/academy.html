Coffee Beans & History

Coffee beans are the seeds found inside the ripe red coffee cherry fruit. They are found all over the world throughout the equatorial region of the globe (called the coffee belt).

Drinking coffee originated almost 500 years ago when it was first discovered in the wild of Ethiopia. No one knows exactly how it started but legend has it that a goat hearder called Khaldi first discovered it when he saw his goats become hyper after eating the coffee cherry.

Today, after the export of the coffee fruit all over the globe, coffee can be found in 3 general regions with distinct flavor profiles. Whit various exceptions, these genreal coffee flavor profiles are as follows:

  • South America: South American coffees are known for having very chocolaty and nutty tastes. These are generally your traditional coffees that most people are used to.
  • Asia Pacific: Asian Pasific Coffees are known for their "spicy" tastes such as cinamon, nutmeg, coreander, ect. A particular popular coffee here is Sumatra, and the yearly favourite Christmas Blend (a barel aged Sumatra coffee)
  • Afric: Africa is very eunique in the coffee world, it is the place where it all started. Most of the coffee in the world comes from 2 original coffee plant types, and therefore while eunique, most coffees will have a similar taste. In africa though, you have various coffee varietals that have never before been categorized (originally lumped into a large group called Heirloom varietals). Coffees from africa will have some of the most fruity tastes in the world, such as blueberry, cherry, and in the case of many Kenyan coffees keylime.

Coffee Roasts

Green Bean: This is the original state of the coffee after it is picked in the fields and processed. It has a yellow, green or tanish collor.

Blonde Roast: Once the coffee begins to roast, it will reach a light brown collor, the coffee here tends to be more citrucy, and has a more plant/ vegetable taste. Individual coffees tend to retain most of their eunique characteristics

Medium Roast: Medium roasts popular around the world tend to balance the brightnes of the coffee with the more developed smokey tastes of a darker roast.

Dark Roast: most of the eunique flavors and acidity are gone, insted you end up with a developed roast that is more smokey and in some cases more bitter than other roasts. Darker roasts are more popular with milk or Espresso based drinks since it's harsher flavors can still be tasted throught the milk and the syrups.
